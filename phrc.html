WELCOME TO PORT HARCOURT REFINING COMPANY LIMITED

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

The plan to build an oil Refinery in Nigeria began in 1954 when the Federal Government granted an oil prospecting and mining licence to Shell-BP. One of the terms of agreement was that as soon as production of Crude oil reached 500,00 barrels per day, a Refinery should be bulit in Nigeria. This target was attained in 1958 and machinery was immediately set in motion to build a refinery at a suitable place.

In 1959, a team of foreign experts arrived in Nigeria to begin preliminary survey for suitable sites along the coastal terrain of the country. In selecting the site, various geographical, economic and soil factors were taken into consideratiob, namely:

  • Nearness to oil field
  • The coasts for sea and inland transportation of the products.
  • A land area firm enough to carry the heavy weight of plant and equipment.
  • An area with relatively slight salt content in the atmosphere which could minimise the danger from corrosion.
From a number of alternative sites in the Lagos and Port Harcourt areas, Alesa-Eleme, near Port Harcourt was chosen as the most suitable .

In December, 1960, a company known as the Shell-BP Petroleum Refining Company of Nigeria Limited with equal share holding by the BP an Shell was formed and charged with the responsibility of constructing the projected refinery.

In 1962 the government of the former Eastern Nigeria leased to the Refining Company the Alesa-Eleme site which lies some distance east of Port Harcourt. That same year, the Federal Government signed a tripartite agreement with the two International Oil Companies and acquired 50% of their shares in the company, leaving the remaining 50% to be shared equally between BP and Shell (Nigeria).

To reflect this tripartite ownership, the name of the company was changed from BP-Shell Petroleum Refining Compnay Limited to the NIGERIAN PETROLEUM REFINING COMPANY LIMITED and later PORT HARCOURT REFINING COMPANY LIMITED with its registered office at Alesa-Eleme.

In 1972, the Federal Government acquired a further 10% share holding, thus bringing the extent of its financial participation in the company to 60%. The balance of 40% share was shared between BP and Shell; each had 20% shares respectively.
The shareholding were as follows:
  • 30% Federal Ministry of Finance Incorporated.
  • 30% State Governments.
  • 40% BP and Shell.
The Shareholders were represented on the Board of Directors bt 6 'A' Directors representing Federal and State Governments;
4 'B' Directors representing the oil Companies. A Nigerian, Chief M.O Feyide was appointed chairman Board of Directors, Finally, in December 1978 the Federal Government through NNPC further acquired the 40% BP and Shell shares in PHRC.
As a result of an Executive Council decision in 1968, NNPC is in the process of acquiring the balance of the 30% shares held by the state governments in PHRC. When adequate compensations have been made to the states, PHRC would eventually be merged with NNPC.


CONSTRUCTION

Construction of the Refinery started in 1963. Situated some 9.6 kilometres down the river due east of Port Harcourt and occupying a total land area of some 300 acres at Alesa-Eleme, the refinery was built at a cost of over 10 million Nigerian Pounds made up of 8 million share capotal and the balance as shareholders loan.

The Company came on stream in 1965 with an initial capacity for refining 35,000 barrels of crude oil per day, making its first shipment of refined products in November, 1965.

However, production was stopped between July, 1967 and September 1969 as a result of the Nigerian Civil War. Considerable damage was done to the plants and equipment during the thirty months civil car.

Between 1969 and 1970, the Federal Government voted the sum of 5 million naira to rehabilitate the damaged plants and equipment. With normal activities resumed, the comoany again shipped the first postwar to Lagos in May, 1970.


OBJECTIVE

PHRC is in business to optimally process hydrocarbon into petroleum products for the benefit of all stakeholders.
The company's vision is to be an innovative International Hydrocarbon Processing Company of choice.

Quality Policy and Objectives

PHRC is committed to always offer petroleum products and service that shall fulfill our customers requirements and expectations using modern technology at proces that guarantee customers value, stake holders continued interest and support.

To meet the objectives of the quality policy, we shall:

  • Continually improve the efficiency of the process and utility plants to optimise their capacity utilization.
  • Deliver all products on specifications.
  • Train at least one third of the total staff population in the relevant skills annually.
  • Strive to achieve nil accident annually.
  • Treat all plant efficients to statutory safe levels before discharge into the environment.
  • Be a responsible corporate citizen by contributing to the social and economic development of our contiguous communities.

PETROLUEM REFINING

Petroleum Refining is a process by which crude oil is separated into several fractions using distillation and other chemical processes.The crude oil produced from underground reservoirs, is a complex mixture of hydrocarbons of varying molecular weights and is of limited use to industries in its raw state. However, when refined or separated into several components, the crude oil produces a wide variety of hydrocarbon materials essential to our daily and modern day requirements.

PHRC Limited consist of two Refineries:

  • The Old Refinery : Commissioned in 1965 with current nameplate capacity of 60,000 barrels per stream day.
  • The New Refinery : Commissioned in 1989 with an installed capacity of 180,000 bspd.

This brings the combined crude processing capacity of the Port Harcourt Refinery to 210,000 bpsd.
It has five (5) process areas » Areas 1-5. The new refinery is made up of Areas 1-4 while the old refinery is Area 5.
