Technical Library

This unit is in charge of information dissemination to the entire refinery.
To carry out most of its functions, ETSD relies on published data. Therefore, it is very important to maintain a Technical Library to procure and retain all relevant publications including reports and books.
The Library's present holdings include materials in a variety of fields covering pure and applied sciences, engineering, industrial processes, business, accounting, computer science and medicine.
These materials are available for the use of PHRC employees who are intrested in brodening their knowledge in these fields. Duties and Responsibilities of a Librarian in this section, includes:

  • They keep current on resources and select publications for the library's collection.
  • They require a knowledgeable command of numerous information sources to select material appropriate to the library's needs. The librarian categorizes, prepares, and catalogs these materials.
  • They also recommend material and help individuals find the information that they need.
  • They analyze and organize collections by subject.
  • They educate individuals on how to use the library systems to find the information they need.
  • Librarians may also provide special classes.


Plant Monitoring



Plant Monitoring is responsible for the routine monitoring of the
performance of the process plants, equipments, and facilities in the Refinery.
These Includes:

  • Crude Oil Distillation Unit (CDU).
  • The Catalytic Reformer Unit (CRU).
  • The Liquified Petroleum Gas Plant (LPG).
  • The Gasoline Blending facilities (GBF).
  • Utitlites, Heat Exchangers, Furnaces and Pumps.
  • Compressors, Tankage, Marketing Area and Jetty Operations.

The routine monitoring is aimed at ensuring a safe, and maximum operating and economic efficiency of the process plants, equipments, and facilities.
It would also aid in technical troubleshooting which is also a responsiblity of the section. Monitoring involves essentially gathering all the pertinent operating data on a routine basis.
Such data include: Operating Temperatures, Pressures and Qualities, compositions and yields of feeds streams, intermediate products and final products. These data areb analysed routinely and potential problem areas highlighted. From these analysis possible solutions to such potential problems are recommended.
Also any observed changes which might lead to more efficient or safer operations are highlighted for incorporation into the standard operating procedure.


Works

This unit is in charge of all projects related to civil structures within the refinery and in the plant. The works unit, not only maintains existing facilities, but also engages in the formulation to completion of minor building/Civil Engineering projects, it partakes in the formulation of briefs for the preparation of the scheme by others.
The activities include:

  • Scrutinizing of schemes prepared.
  • Working with others to prepare and present to Management suitable proposals for consideration and execution.
  • Preparation of specifications, Bill of Quantities and other related tender documents for major works to be carried out by contrect.
  • Supervision of Contractors on major works to ensure that the contract works are carried out in accordance with the specifications.


Projects

This unit is into ensuring that Project Management processes are adhered to in execution of project in PHRC. Handles execution and monitoring of projects, to ensure that pre-defined project requirements are met. As required by ISO 9001:2008, the section is guided by a standard operative procedures for project management and engineering design. Projects handled can be in-hand projects (using in-hand capabilities) or third-party projects (third-party executed or awarded projects). The unit is headed by a Superintendent Project. Engineer W.S. Opusunju. Stages of project management include:

  • Initiation : Indentifying the project and specific project requirement. And obtaining formal management approval.
  • Planning : Developing a plan for monitoring, controlling and executing project. Carrying out stakeholders analysis to determine stakeholders expectation and requirement for the projects so as to develop a detailed scope of work and cost estimate for the project.
  • Execution and Control : This stage begins with a project kick-off meeting of all project stakeholders. And also monitoring the progress of the projecr through regular progress review meeting with relevant Stakeholders.
    It also involves identification, tracking and control of changes to the project scope and obtaining management approval for any relevant change to the initial project requirement.
  • Close Out Stage : At this stage, you ensure that all components of the project has been completed and signed.
    This involves meeting with all relevant stakeholders in a project close out meeting so as to produce project close out report deatailing lessons learnt and any pending issues for future actions.
    Examples of recent project carried out by the unit includes:
    » Rehabilitation of Demineralized water plant in Power Plant Utilities (PPU).
    » Rehabilitation of crude oil storage tank.
    » Provision of alternative crude oil receipt line. Using marine vessels to solve the problem of supply of crude.      Neccessitated by incessant oil pipline.
